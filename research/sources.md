# Annotated source library

Links marked **open access** provide full text. DOI/PubMed links may lead to an abstract or publisher access page.

## Wearable measurement

1. Lee YJ et al. *Performance of consumer wrist-worn sleep tracking devices compared to polysomnography: a meta-analysis.* 2025. **Open access.** Pooled differences and high heterogeneity; supports trend use rather than PSG equivalence. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11874098/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5664/jcsm.11460)
2. Schyvens A-M et al. *A performance validation of six commercial wrist-worn wearable sleep-tracking devices for sleep stage scoring compared to polysomnography.* 2025. **Open access.** Sleep sensitivity exceeded 90%, but wake specificity was 29.39%–52.15%. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12038347/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/sleepadvances/zpaf021)
3. Agostinho M et al. *Are Wearable Sleep-Tracking Devices Reliable Alternatives to Polysomnography? A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.* 2026. Finds overestimation of sleep and underestimation of wake after sleep onset with device variability. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42175611/)
4. Imtiaz SA. *A Systematic Review of Sensing Technologies for Wearable Sleep Staging.* Sensors. 2021;21:1562. **Open access.** Explains PPG, accelerometry, EEG, and staging limitations. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7956647/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3390/s21051562)
5. Khosla S et al. *Consumer sleep technology: an American Academy of Sleep Medicine position statement.* J Clin Sleep Med. 2018;14:877–880. Consumer technology should not replace diagnosis/treatment without validation and clearance. [AASM statement](https://aasm.org/advocacy/position-statements/consumer-sleep-technology/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5664/jcsm.7128)

## Light and circadian effects

6. Mason IC et al. *Light exposure during sleep impairs cardiometabolic function.* PNAS. 2022;119:e2113290119. **Open access.** Controlled crossover study (n=20) comparing dim and moderate room light during sleep. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8944904/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2113290119)
7. Cho YM et al. *Effects of artificial light at night on human health: a literature review of observational and experimental studies applied to exposure assessment.* Chronobiology International. 2015. Background on spectrum, intensity, timing, and circadian responses. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3109/07420528.2015.1073158)
8. Tähkämö L et al. *Systematic review of light exposure impact on human circadian rhythm.* Chronobiology International. 2019;36:151–170. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30311830/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/07420528.2018.1527773)
9. Shechter A et al. *Interventions to reduce short-wavelength (“blue”) light exposure at night and their effects on sleep: a systematic review and meta-analysis.* Sleep Advances. 2020;1:zpaa002. **Open access.** Possible benefits, with heterogeneous methods. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37192881/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/sleepadvances/zpaa002)
10. Chambe J et al. *Light therapy in insomnia disorder: a systematic review and meta-analysis.* Journal of Sleep Research. 2023;32:e13895. Some effectiveness for sleep maintenance; timing changes phase in different directions. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37002704/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/jsr.13895)
11. Thompson A et al. *Effects of dawn simulation on markers of sleep inertia and post-waking performance in humans.* European Journal of Applied Physiology. 2014;114:1049–1056. Small crossover study (n=8). [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24509892/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00421-014-2831-z)

## Sound, masking, and acoustic stimulation

12. Riedy SM et al. *Noise as a sleep aid: A systematic review.* Sleep Medicine Reviews. 2021;55:101385. Evidence for continuous noise was rated very low quality. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33007706/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.smrv.2020.101385)
13. Capezuti E et al. *Systematic review: auditory stimulation and sleep.* J Clin Sleep Med. 2022;18:1697–1709. **Open access.** 34 studies, 1,103 participants; no strong evidence for benefit. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9163611/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5664/jcsm.9860)
14. Basner M et al. *Efficacy of pink noise and earplugs for mitigating the effects of intermittent environmental noise exposure on sleep.* Sleep. 2026;49:zsag001. **Open access.** Randomized laboratory crossover (n=25); continuous pink noise reduced REM in a dose-dependent pattern, while earplugs performed better under tested conditions. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13163165/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/sleep/zsag001)
15. Wunderlin M et al. *Modulating overnight memory consolidation by acoustic stimulation during slow-wave sleep: a systematic review and meta-analysis.* Sleep. 2021;44:zsaa296. CLAS research; effects are not equivalent to continuous white noise. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33406249/) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/sleep/zsaa296)
16. Jafarzadeh Esfahani M et al. *Closed-loop auditory stimulation of sleep slow oscillations: Basic principles and best practices.* Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews. 2023;153:105379. Technical review of EEG phase targeting and experimental design. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37660843/) · [open manuscript](https://repository.essex.ac.uk/36526/1/1-s2.0-S0149763423003482-main.pdf) · [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2023.105379)
17. Basner M et al. *Environmental Noise and Effects on Sleep: An Update to the WHO Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.* Environmental Health Perspectives. 2022. **Open access.** Establishes environmental noise as a sleep disruptor; distinct from evidence for adding masking noise. [Full text](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9272916/)

## Health, noise, and professional guidance

18. World Health Organization. *Noise* fact sheet. Recommends less than 30 dB(A) indoors in bedrooms at night for good-quality sleep. [WHO](https://www.who.int/Europe/news-room/fact-sheets/item/noise)
19. World Health Organization Regional Office for Europe. *Night noise guidelines for Europe.* 2009. [Publication and PDF](https://www.who.int/europe/publications/i/item/9789289041737)
20. American Academy of Sleep Medicine. *Clinical use of a home sleep apnea test: updated position statement.* 2025 web update. Automated consumer data alone should not determine diagnosis or treatment. [AASM](https://aasm.org/advocacy/position-statements/clinical-use-of-a-home-sleep-apnea-test-an-updated-american-academy-of-sleep-medicine-position-statement/)
